about Mother's Day

Mother's Day is celebrated in India with great enthusiasm and love, just like in many other parts of the world. It is a day dedicated to honoring and expressing gratitude to mothers for their unconditional love and sacrifices. In India, Mother's Day is celebrated on the second Sunday of May, just like in the United States and other countries. On this day, people express their love and appreciation for their mothers in various ways, such as giving them gifts, flowers, cards, or taking them out for a special meal. Religious and cultural festivals are also a big part of Mother's Day celebrations in India. For example, in Hinduism, the divine mother is worshipped as a symbol of love, compassion, and sacrifice. Many people visit temples or participate in religious ceremonies to seek blessings for their mothers on this special day. Overall, Mother's Day is an important occasion for Indians to show their love and respect for their mothers, and to acknowledge the important role that mothers play in the lives of their families and communities.

A mother's love is like no other,

It's pure and gentle, like a feather.

It's warm and soft, like a summer breeze,

And it's strong enough to move mountains with ease.

A mother's love is patient and kind,

It's selfless and giving, without a thought in mind.

It's a constant presence, a guiding light,

And it shines through the darkest of nights.

A mother's love is a gift from above,

A precious treasure, full of love.

It's a bond that nothing can sever,

And it lasts forever and ever.

So to all the mothers out there,

I want to say how much I care.

Thank you for all that you do,

And for the love that shines through.
